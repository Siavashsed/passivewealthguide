## QUORA
**Answer this question:** How long does it typically take to make real money from selling digital products?

Based on my experience launching digital products, here's the honest timeline:

Months 1-3: Don't expect consistency. I sold a PDF budgeting workbook for $17 and made $51 in month one. Month two was worse. People often see this initial traction and think it'll compound immediately—it doesn't.

Months 4-7: This is the flatline period. Your product exists, you've proven there's demand, but growth stalls. Most creators quit here because it feels like failure. It's not—it's typically the phase where you need to actively market, improve based on feedback, or iterate the product itself.

Months 8-12: Momentum builds if you've been consistent. Reviews accumulate. Word-of-mouth starts working. You begin understanding what actually resonates with your audience versus what you thought would.

Months 13-18: By here, you're either seeing real traction or you've learned why this particular product won't work. Both outcomes are valuable.

The uncomfortable truth? "Real money" depends on your definition. I wasn't making meaningful passive income until month 12-14. By month 18, I had systems working. But that required treating it like a business during the invisible months, not ignoring it until results appeared.

If you're considering this path, plan for 18 months of inconsistent returns while you figure out what works. That's not failure—that's the actual cost of building passive income.
