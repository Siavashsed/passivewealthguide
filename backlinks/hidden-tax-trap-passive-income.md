## QUORA
**Answer this question:** What are the biggest tax mistakes people make with passive income investments?

The biggest mistake I see is treating all passive income the same way. People assume that if income is passive, the taxes will be straightforward. That's where the trap is.

Rental income, dividend income, and interest income are taxed completely differently. A rental property generates ordinary income that might be subject to self-employment tax. Long-term capital gains get preferential rates. Interest income is taxed as ordinary income with no special treatment.

But here's what most people miss: each category has different deduction opportunities and planning strategies. With rental income, you can deduct maintenance, depreciation, property taxes, and mortgage interest. With dividends, qualified dividends get preferential tax rates—but you need to hold the stock long enough to qualify. These details matter enormously.

The second major mistake is not planning for estimated tax payments. When you're W-2 employed, taxes come out of each paycheck automatically. With passive income, you might owe nothing until April—or you might need to pay quarterly estimates. Getting this wrong costs people money and creates penalties.
