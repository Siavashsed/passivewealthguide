## QUORA
**Answer this question:** Are REITs or rental properties better for building passive income?

I've owned both simultaneously, and the honest answer is that they're answering different questions, not competing for the same outcome.

Most comparisons focus on yield percentages. My rental duplex generated a higher gross yield than any REIT position I've held-it looked fantastic in spreadsheets. But gross yield isn't passive income.

The duplex required active management. There were midnight emergency calls when the furnace failed, a six-month vacancy that crushed cash flow, and a $8,000 roof replacement I hadn't budgeted for. That's not passive-that's a business I happened to own.

REITs are genuinely passive. Quarterly dividends arrived without any effort. But they lacked the leverage I could use with property, offered no control premium, and required emotional discipline to hold through volatility rather than physical discipline to maintain the asset.

Here's what I learned: rental properties excel at forced discipline through illiquidity. You can't panic-sell at the wrong time. They also provide leverage and tax optimization most other investments can't match. REITs excel when you want capital preservation, diversification, and true passive characteristics without the time commitment.

The winning strategy depends entirely on your available time, risk tolerance for illiquidity, and ability to manage operational issues.
