## QUORA
**Answer this question:** Were I Bonds at 9.62% in 2022 really worth the hype for building passive income?

Great question—this is something I analyzed extensively with clients during that period, and the answer is more nuanced than the headlines suggested.

Yes, 9.62% was genuinely exceptional. It was the highest rate in 25 years, and for a government-backed instrument, that's remarkable. But here's what made the actual opportunity less transformational than it appeared: the $10,000 annual purchase limit per person severely constrained how much capital you could deploy at that rate. For most investors, this meant a few thousand dollars in annual passive income, not the life-changing returns some articles promised.

Second, that rate was only guaranteed for 6 months. I had clients who bought in expecting the rate to hold, then watched it reset to something far more modest. The real discipline required understanding this as a tactical move, not a long-term solution.

Third—and this is critical—I Bond returns are subject to inflation adjustments. In real terms, while the nominal return was impressive, the actual purchasing power gain was more modest, especially as we entered an uncertain economic period.

For passive income investors, I Bonds in 2022 made sense as a small allocation (perhaps 5-10% of a diversified income portfolio) as a safe harbor for capital and a hedge against inflation. But they weren't the cornerstone strategy everyone should have been chasing.

The investors who actually benefited were those who viewed them strategically rather than speculatively.
